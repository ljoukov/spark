## (2)

Let the middle of \(AB\) be \(C\) as shown.  
Now to get from \(A\) to \(B\), you must go through \(C\).

Now there are 7 ways to get to \(C\) from \(A\):

\[
A \to D \to C,\quad
A \to C,\quad
A \to G \to C,\quad
A \to D \to C \to A \to G \to C,\quad
A \to G \to C \to A \to D \to C,
\]
\[
A \to D \to C' \to G \to A' \to C,\quad
A \to G \to C \to D \to A \to C
\]

Now when she gets to \(C\), there are only 3 ways to get to \(B\),  
as she stops at \(B\) the first time she gets there.

This gives:
\[
7 \times 3 = 21\ \text{ways}
\]
